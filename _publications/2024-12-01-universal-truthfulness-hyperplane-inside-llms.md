This paper explores the concept of a universal truthfulness hyperplane within Large Language Models (LLMs). We investigate how truthfulness manifests in model representations and identify consistent patterns across different models and tasks.

**Key Contributions:**
1. Identification of a universal truthfulness hyperplane in LLM representations
2. Analysis of truthfulness patterns across different models and tasks
3. Demonstration of consistent truthfulness representations

The research provides insights into how LLMs internally represent truthfulness and how these representations can be leveraged for improved model performance on truth-related tasks. We show that despite architectural differences, LLMs exhibit consistent patterns in how they encode truthfulness information.
