This paper proposes using in-context sharpness as alerts for hallucination mitigation in language models. We analyze inner representation patterns to identify potential hallucination instances before they occur in model outputs.

**Key Contributions:**
1. Introduction of in-context sharpness as an early warning signal for hallucinations
2. Analysis of inner representation patterns associated with hallucination
3. Framework for proactive hallucination mitigation

The research provides a novel perspective on addressing the hallucination problem in language models by examining internal representations rather than just output patterns. We demonstrate that sharpness measures in contextual representations can serve as reliable indicators of potential hallucination events.
